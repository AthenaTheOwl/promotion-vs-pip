# Rules — v0

A first-session paper prototype. 3–5 players. ~60 minutes. No engine, no scorekeeping app — bring a notepad.

## Setup (5 minutes)

1. Print `print.html` on cardstock or cut from regular paper. 36 cards across 4 pages.
2. Separate by type:
   - 6 Meetings (blue border)
   - 18 Proposals (green)
   - 6 Policies (orange)
   - 6 Exposures (red)
3. Shuffle each pile.
4. Deal to each player: 5 Proposals + 2 Exposures.
5. Lay all 6 Policies face-up in the middle — these are a shared pool.
6. On the notepad, give each player two tracks: **Reputation** (starts 0) and **Red Flags** (starts 0).

## Round structure (6 rounds)

Each round runs through these steps in order:

1. **Reveal a Meeting.** Flip the top Meeting card. The player on dealer's left reads the card aloud and performs the alienation. The Meeting's effect applies to the rest of the round.

2. **Propose.** Going clockwise, each player may play one Proposal from their hand. Read the title and effect, then perform the alienation.

3. **Audience vote.** After each Proposal, the other players vote thumbs-up or thumbs-down. The card's effect names what each vote does (most score +reputation on a positive majority).

4. **Resolve.** Apply the effect. Update Reputation and Red Flag counters on the notepad.

5. **Expose (optional).** Any player may play one Exposure on a target. Read the card. Apply.

6. **Refill.** Draw back up to 5 Proposals + 2 Exposures.

## Win conditions

- **Promotion** — at the end of round 6, the player with the highest Reputation wins promotion. Tied players each give a 30-second acceptance speech; the audience picks.

- **PIP** — any player who hits 4 Red Flags before round 6 ends is placed on a Performance Improvement Plan. They flip their player card: they now play as HR, can play Exposures freely, and cannot win the promotion. If two players hit PIP, the game ends with no winner ("hiring freeze").

## House rules / soft variations

- **Hammy reads count.** If the alienation prompt earns a laugh, the player gets +1 Reputation. If it earns a groan, +1 Red Flag.
- **No table-talk** during Proposals. Reactions only.
- **Time limit per Proposal**: 90 seconds, then the table votes regardless.

## After the session

Fill in `playtest-log/<date>.md`. The repo's first playtest template names what to capture: what broke, what worked, edits-for-next.
